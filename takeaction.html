1. Consume less

Consuming and buying less is the most acheivable and easy action item. You may think that not buying a pair of jeans or shoes doesn't make a big impact on the environment in the great scheme of things, but a little goes a long way. By encouraging friends and family to work with the clothes they already have, or invest in more durable garnments, change can happen. Support the FABRIC Act S4213

The Fabric Act is a federal bill introduced in Congress that aims to:

1. Establish a nationwide garment industry registry

2. Set minimum wage standards for workers

3. Hold brands and retailers responsible for workplace violations

4. Implement a $40 million domestic garment manufacturing grant program

5. Enact 30% reshoring tax credit to move manufacturing operations to the US
